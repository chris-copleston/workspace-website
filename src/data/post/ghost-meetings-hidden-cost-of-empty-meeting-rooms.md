Your office calendar says every meeting room is booked.

Your employees say they can't find anywhere to meet.

Yet a quick walk around the office reveals several meeting rooms sitting empty.

These are **ghost meetings** — rooms that have been reserved but are never used. While they may seem like a minor inconvenience, ghost meetings have become one of the biggest hidden costs in today's workplace, frustrating employees, reducing productivity and preventing organisations from getting the most from their office investment.

## The Real Cost of Ghost Meetings

As organisations continue encouraging employees back into the office, collaboration spaces have become some of the most valuable assets within the workplace. When those spaces remain booked but unused, the impact extends far beyond an empty room.

| The Challenge | The Business Impact |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Rooms remain booked despite no attendees | Valuable office space sits idle while other teams struggle to find somewhere to collaborate. |
| Employees spend time searching for available rooms | Productivity falls and frustration increases across the workforce. |
| Meetings are delayed or relocated | Decisions take longer, projects slow down and customer responsiveness suffers. |
| Catering, IT and Facilities continue supporting cancelled meetings | Organisations incur unnecessary operational costs and wasted resources. |
| Employees lose confidence in the booking system | Overbooking and duplicate bookings become common, making availability even worse. |

Over time, these small inefficiencies accumulate into thousands of lost productive hours, unnecessary operational expenses and a workplace experience that feels far less efficient than it should.

## Why Traditional Booking Tools Aren't Enough

Applications such as Microsoft Outlook are excellent for scheduling meetings, but they were never designed to manage workplaces.

They can reserve a meeting room, but they cannot determine whether anyone actually arrived, automatically release unused spaces, notify catering that a meeting has been cancelled or inform IT that support is no longer required.

In today's hybrid workplace, simply booking a room is no longer enough. Organisations need a platform that actively manages the entire meeting lifecycle — from reservation through to room utilisation.
